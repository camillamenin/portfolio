THE HABIT TRACKER

What one thing I could do that, if I

started

stopped

doing on a regular basis, would make a tremendous positive difference in my life?

ABOUT THE PROJECT

This project started in December 2020: I was enjoying the holiday festivities with close friends and family, indulging in all the little pleasures and drinking almost every night because, well, it's Christmas!


After the holidays I was determined to come back to a healthier lifestyle, to reduce my sugar intake as well as alcoholic drinks, and increase healthy habits like exercise, yoga, and meditation.


I knew I could change a single habit if I maintained it for 21 days straight, but I was ambitious: I wanted to create a method that would improve multiple habits at the same time. I set my goal at 10 different habits: some were part of a daily routine, some were on a weekly basis.


Given the scope of the project, I was going to be kind on myself: positive behaviors were rewarded and little indulgences were tolerated. In the end, this was an attempt to improve my life and create a useful tool, not a way to punish my human cravings.


Could I reach my goals in just three months? Turns out yes, it's possible.

  • Date: Dec 2020 - March 2021
  • Client: Personal Project
  • Category: Human-Centered Design, Visual Design, Data Analysis, UX Research, Layout Design
  • Tools: Sketch, Adobe Photoshop, Notion, Evernote, pen&paper

How might we design a system to support good habits and discourage bad ones?

THE HABITS

UNHEALTHY HABITS

To reduce:

  • Sugar to 2-3 times/week.
  • Alcohol to 2-3 drinks/week.

HEALTHY HABITS

To Increase:

  • Yoga to 3 times a week.
  • Have a daily workout.
  • Have a daily meditation.
  • Have a daily cold shower.

SOCIAL HABITS

  • Stay connected with my friends and family on a weekly basis.
  • Keep track of the quality time with my partner.

CREATIVE HABITS

Make time every week for

  • Writing.
  • Sketching.
  • Cooking

METHODS CONSIDERED

CHECKLISTS

A simple tool for short lists of less than 10 small items. It worked best with daily hours when my day was particularly busy: I would combine my daily tasks with the habits and surprisingly everything was done by the end of the day.

ROUTINES

Most people love structure, consistency, and completing a series of easy yet satisfying tasks. I combined my daily meditation, workout, and cold shower into an effective morning ritual that helped me jump start my day (and make my bed as well).

TRACKING TOOL

Simple and colorful stickers helped me keep track of my habits every week: it worked wonders, as the visual clues made it easy to notice my progress. The unhealthy habits went down and the positive ones jumped up.

RESULTS

At the end of every month the progress was clear: I was visibly increasing the healthy habits and decreasing the negative behaviors. With the simple aid of colorful markers, it became increasingly easier to follow my new habits.


By the beginning of April, the result was striking: I had successfully reached my goals. There were clearly days of highs and days of lows, but even then, by kindly moving on and not stressing about every single thing, I had assumed a positive behavior in all the habits I wanted to establish. A huge win!
